2. The Master Template (Step-by-Step)

Memorize this structure. It works for Opinion, Argument, and Description tasks.

PHASE 2: The Reasoning (0:15–0:40)

"The most compelling justification for this stance is that [Point 1]. To be more precise, this strategy facilitates [Positive Result]."

PHASE 3: The Example (0:40–0:65) [CRITICAL]

PHASE 4: The Pivot (0:65–0:80)

"Admittedly, some detractors might argue that [Counter-Point]. However, I contend that the benefits inevitably outweigh the drawbacks."

PHASE 5: The Conclusion (0:80–0:90)

"Consequently, based on these factors, it is imperative that we prioritize [Opinion]."

5. The 5-5-5-5 Vocab Arsenal

Memorize these words. Use them in EVERY answer.

5 Verbs (Action)

  • Facilitate (help)
    "The new software will facilitate smoother communication between departments."
  • Foster (build/create)
    "Teachers aim to foster a sense of curiosity in their students."
  • Mitigate (reduce/stop)
    "Planting trees helps to mitigate the negative effects of pollution."
  • Underscore (highlight)
    "Recent studies underscore the importance of regular exercise."
  • Entail (involve)
    "This project will entail significant research and development efforts."

5 Adjectives (Description)

  • Detrimental (bad/harmful)
    "A lack of sleep can be detrimental to your cognitive health."
  • Imperative (important)
    "It is imperative that we address climate change immediately."
  • Pivotal (key/main)
    "Education plays a pivotal role in a nation's economic growth."
  • Substantial (big)
    "We have observed a substantial increase in renewable energy usage."
  • Profound (deep)
    "The internet has had a profound impact on social interaction."

5 Adverbs (Intensity)

  • Predominantly (mostly)
    "The population in this region is predominantly rural."
  • Inevitably (will happen)
    "Technological progress inevitably leads to changes in the job market."
  • Undeniably (truly)
    "Clean water is undeniably essential for public health."
  • Admittedly (concession)
    "Admittedly, the initial cost is high, but the long-term benefits are great."
  • Drastically (a lot)
    "Smartphone usage has drastically altered how we communicate."

5 Nouns (Concepts)

  • Repercussion (result)
    "The economic repercussions of the policy were unexpected."
  • Endeavor (effort/activity)
    "Space exploration is a challenging scientific endeavor."
  • Consensus (agreement)
    "The committee finally reached a consensus on the budget."
  • Discrepancy (difference)
    "There was a noticeable discrepancy between the two witness accounts."
  • Proficiency (skill)
    "Achieving proficiency in a new language requires consistent practice."

4. The Panic Button: Future Prediction

Problem: You finish your story at 60 seconds. You have 30 seconds left. You are panic-stricken.
Solution: Do not repeat yourself. Shift to the FUTURE tense.

The "Future Prediction" Formula

Use this exact sentence structure to fill 15-20 seconds with high-level vocabulary:

"Looking ahead, I predict that if this trend continues, we will see substantial changes in the way society approaches [Topic]. Specifically, it will likely lead to [Prediction]."

Examples:

  • Topic (Environment): "Looking ahead, I predict that if we ignore climate change, we will see drastic alterations in global weather patterns. Specifically, it will likely lead to severe economic instability."
  • Topic (Education): "Looking ahead, I predict that if technology integration continues, we will see a profound shift in how students learn. Specifically, it will likely lead to the end of traditional textbooks."
